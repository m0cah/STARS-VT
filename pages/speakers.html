Guest Speakers

Colonel Norsky

Col Charlie Norsky is currently the Chief of the Integration Division on the United States Space Force Headquarters Staff. Most recently, he completed a tour in the Office of the Secretary of Defense for Space Policy, where he led the Space Control Policy Branch for the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Space Policy. Col Norsky entered the Air Force in 2002 as a distinguished graduate of the Air Force ROTC program at Virginia Tech in Blacksburg, Virginia. Throughout his early career, he has served in a variety of positions at the squadron and group levels in missile warning operations, training, evaluation, operational testing, and weapons and tactics. Col Norsky has also served with Combined Joint Task Force One as a Space Liaison Officer in Afghanistan, providing space effects to more than 30,000 combat troops in theater. He is a graduate of the U.S. Air Force Weapons School, the School of Advanced Air and Space Studies, and the National War College. Col Norsky also completed his squadron command tour as the Commander of the 2d Space Operations Squadron, Schriever Space Force Base, Colorado. General Thompson

David Dean "DT" Thompson (born February 18, 1963) is a United States Space Force general currently serving as the service's first vice chief of space operations. He previously served as the vice commander of the United States Space Force, a position he retained from the Air Force Space Command when it was established as a separate service branch, from April 2, 2018, to September 30, 2020. He assumed his current assignment on October 2, 2020.

Colonel Dorminey

Colonel Eric S. Dorminey is the is the Director, Information Mobility, Space Operations Command where he leverages over 100 personnel and over $100 billion dollars in space assets to generate, present and sustain United States Space Force Missile Warning, Wissile Track, Military Satellite Communications and Precision Navigation and Timing systems. Colonel Dorminey graduated from the Virginia Tech with a Mechanical Engineering degree and received his commission through Reserve Officer Training Corps in 1990. Colonel Dorminey is a master space operator with command and control qualifications in Missile Warning, Space Surveillance and Satellite Command and Control systems. In addition to his many space operations and staff assignments, he has served in a variety of deployed locations including Iraq, Afghanistan and in support of the Marines in Operation Enduring Freedom-Philippines.
